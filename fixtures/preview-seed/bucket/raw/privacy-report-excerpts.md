# Privacy Report Excerpts

Reference: [Nielsen Norman Group - Design for Privacy](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/privacy/).

Users trusted the product more when they could point to where data lived, what left the device, and what still worked offline. The strongest reactions were not about encryption jargon; they were about visible boundaries and predictable failure handling.

Repeated patterns from the report:

- People calm down when storage location is explicit.
- Sync feels safer when it is legible, reversible, and not overly magical.
- Permission language matters most when the app is about to cross a boundary.

The report repeatedly connected trust to predictability rather than branding. That is a useful lens for [[Trust Signals]], [[Local First Principles]], and the Maple Clinic notes.
