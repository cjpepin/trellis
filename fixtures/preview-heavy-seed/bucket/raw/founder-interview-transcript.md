# Founder Interview

Reference: [First Round Review - founder-led sales playbook](https://review.firstround.com/the-founder-led-sales-playbook-for-early-stage-startups/).

The most useful line in this interview was not about selling harder. It was about keeping one promise visible long enough that customers can repeat it back in their own words. That maps closely to [[Founder OS]] and [[Product Council]] because both notes are really about protecting a narrow story from quarter-to-quarter drift.

Working takeaways:
- Treat design partner conversations as evidence, not decoration.
- Name the next quarter in one sentence before naming the project list.
- Make the operating rhythm observable enough that new teammates can inherit it quickly.

This source is worth revisiting whenever the roadmap starts to widen faster than the team can explain it.
